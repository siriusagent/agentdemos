Hepatopancreatobiliary research

Hepatopancreatobiliary (HPB) research covers diseases and interventions involving the liver, pancreas, biliary tree, and gallbladder. The latest PubMed sample retrieved here is dominated by cancer biology, early detection, immunotherapy resistance, metabolic liver disease, and minimally invasive/endoscopic treatment.

The center of gravity is translational: blood biomarkers for earlier detection, microenvironment biology for therapy resistance, and procedure standardization for complex HPB operations.

Current directions

  • Liquid biopsy and methylation assays

    Blood-based signatures are moving from broad promise into named assay formats: dPCR FAR1 methylation for early HCC detection, and review-level coverage of ctDNA, methylation, exosomes, and circulating tumor cells across PDAC, HCC, and CCA.

  • Immune resistance in PDAC and HCC

    New papers emphasize tumor microenvironment mechanisms: sex-linked NK-cell differences in PDAC, KRAS-driven PDIA6 suppression of immunogenic cell death, and neoantigen studies after combined radioimmunotherapy in HCC.

  • Molecular targets in biliary and liver cancer

    ICC and HCC reports cluster around ferroptosis, PI3K/AKT/mTOR-CDK2, m6A methylation, p38 apoptosis, and cell-cycle control — mostly preclinical but useful for mapping where translational energy is going.

  • Metabolism, microbiome, and MASH-to-HCC risk

    Recent work keeps connecting diet, microbes, cholesterol, saturated fat, MASH fibrosis, hepatitis B, cardiometabolic risk, and downstream HCC surveillance decisions.

  • Minimally invasive and endoscopic HPB care

    Procedure-focused HPB work includes EUS-guided hepaticogastrostomy maturation evidence and ongoing standardization of robotic/laparoscopic HPB programs.

Clinical signal

Nearer-term signal: biomarker validation, perioperative PDAC regimen comparisons, cost-effectiveness for systemic HCC therapy, and MIS HPB implementation requirements.

Earlier-stage signal: many oncology papers remain cell-line, animal, single-cohort, or computational studies. They map candidate mechanisms but should not be read as practice-changing without prospective validation.
