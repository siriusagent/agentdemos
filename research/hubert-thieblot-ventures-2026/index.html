Timeline

From guild utility to scaled platform to venture seat.

The public record is cleanest when read as a sequence of operating chapters. Each one compounds credibility for the next.

2005

Curse starts as a user-made tool.

Curse-Gaming.com launched as a World of Warcraft add-on database, rooted in tooling Thieblot and his guild were already using. This matters because the company's DNA was utility first, not media first.

  • Origin story tied to guild infrastructure and mod distribution.
  • Formal incorporation followed in 2006.
2007–2012

Funding, relocation, and category expansion.

Curse raised outside capital, shifted toward San Francisco, and expanded beyond add-ons into sites, databases, forums, and software. By 2012, Forbes described the business at more than 125 workers and more than 23 million monthly viewers.

  • Curse also earned Microsoft BizSpark One and Inc. 500 recognition.
  • Thieblot was named an Ernst & Young Entrepreneur Of The Year finalist in Northern California.
2013–2016

Curse becomes a wider gaming operating system.

The product stack widened: Curse Client, CurseForge, Gamepedia, and Curse Voice. The company was no longer just a destination website. It was becoming infrastructure for gaming communities and creators.

  • Media, mod tooling, wikis, and voice under one umbrella.
  • A stronger signal of platform thinking than simple audience aggregation.
2016–2018

Twitch acquisition and operator-after-exit phase.

Variety reported Twitch's acquisition of Curse in August 2016. Public biographies then place Thieblot in a vice president role at Twitch for roughly two years following the deal.

  • The exit validated the scale and strategic relevance of Curse.
  • The post-acquisition stint suggests he saw integration from inside the buyer as well.
2022–now

Founders, Inc. and the investor chapter.

His current highest-confidence public role is General Partner at Founders, Inc. That position is the clearest public expression of his move from builder to backer.

  • Founders, Inc. publicly lists him on its team page.
  • Secondary aggregators place him there since April 2022.

How to read him

The founder-impression angle is operator substance, not polished theater.

If your goal is to impress him, the public record suggests he will respect evidence of real building more than generic venture language.

What likely resonates

Utility born from real user pain, community-native distribution, product obsession, fast iteration, and founder density.

What likely falls flat

Abstract market-speak, overly polished status signaling, and “venture-ready” language with no proof of product truth.

Best one-line framing

Founder-turned-investor who scaled a gaming infrastructure business before moving into capital.
