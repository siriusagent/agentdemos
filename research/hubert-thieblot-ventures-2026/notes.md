# Hubert Thieblot research notes

## Executive summary
Hubert Thieblot reads as an operator-first investor rather than a pure finance-brand VC. The strongest sourced story is: he built Curse from a World of Warcraft guild utility into a large gaming media and tooling platform, sold it to Twitch in 2016, spent time inside Twitch after the acquisition, and now serves as General Partner at Founders, Inc. That arc—community product instinct, gaming infrastructure, scaling, exit, then founder-facing investing—is likely the most credible angle for impressing him.

## Why he matters
- Built **Curse** out of a real user problem in gaming communities rather than starting with a financial thesis.
- Scaled Curse into a multi-property gaming network with tens of millions of monthly users.
- Operated across multiple layers of the gaming stack: media, mod tooling, wikis, voice, and esports adjacency.
- Transitioned from founder/operator to investor and platform-builder at **Founders, Inc.**
- Maintains an active public presence on **X (@hthieblot)**, making founder-facing public positioning especially relevant.

## Timeline
### 2005–2006: origin of Curse
- Curse-Gaming.com launched publicly in January 2005 as a World of Warcraft add-on database, growing out of tooling used by Thieblot's guild.
- Curse was formally incorporated in 2006.

### 2007–2012: financing and scale-up
- Curse raised early outside funding, including a 2007 Series A and a 2009 Series B, then expanded beyond add-ons into a wider gaming network.
- By late 2012, Forbes described Curse as having over **125 workers** and over **23 million monthly viewers**.
- In 2012, Ernst & Young named Thieblot a Northern California Entrepreneur Of The Year finalist.

### 2013–2016: broader platform ambition
- Curse expanded into products including **Curse Client**, **CurseForge**, **Gamepedia**, and **Curse Voice**.
- The business increasingly looked like gaming infrastructure and community operating system, not just a fan site network.

### 2016–2018: Twitch chapter
- Twitch announced it would acquire Curse in August 2016.
- Public biographies state Thieblot then served at Twitch in a vice president role for roughly two years after the deal.

### 2022–present: Founders, Inc.
- Founders, Inc. identifies Thieblot as **General Partner** on its team page.
- Secondary profile aggregators describe him as leading investments there since April 2022.

## Ventures and products most associated with him
### 1. Curse
The central company in his story. Curse began as a World of Warcraft add-on utility and evolved into a large gaming network spanning content, communities, databases, and software tools.

### 2. Curse Client
Desktop software for managing game mods/add-ons, broadening Curse from media and hosting into utility software.

### 3. CurseForge
A mod-hosting and developer platform that outlived the original company and later moved under Overwolf after Twitch's ownership period.

### 4. Gamepedia
Wiki-hosting infrastructure for gaming communities, showing Thieblot's repeated interest in user-generated knowledge systems.

### 5. Curse Voice
Voice communications product for gamers, representing a push into live social infrastructure rather than static content.

### 6. Founders, Inc.
His current public institutional platform. The firm presents itself as deeply founder-oriented; Thieblot is listed as General Partner on the team page.
